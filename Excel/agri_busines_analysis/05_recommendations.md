# Section 5 — Recommendations

[← Back to README](../README.md)

---

Based on the analysis across all four dashboards and 19 business questions, the following actionable recommendations are proposed for the Agri Startup.

---

## (a) Target Medium Farmers as the Core Segment

Medium farmers (2–5 acres) generate ~48–52% of all revenue despite being 36% of the customer base. They are the highest-value segment.

**Actions:**
- Design marketing campaigns specifically for medium farmers in Karnataka and Tamil Nadu
- Offer bundled packages (Polyhouse + Fertigation + Consultancy) with structured EMI options
- Create a dedicated relationship manager programme for medium farmers in top districts

---

## (b) Use Consultancy as the Entry Product

Consultancy services have low direct revenue (~₹7,500–₹1,50,000 per engagement) but consistently precede large infrastructure orders.

**Actions:**
- Offer a subsidised or free first consultancy session to new farmers
- Build a structured upsell pathway: Consultancy → Net House → Polyhouse → Hi-Tech Polyhouse
- Track consultancy-to-infrastructure conversion rates as a key sales KPI

---

## (c) Expand into Underserved States

Telangana (7–9% of projects) and Madhya Pradesh (18–20%) are underpenetrated relative to their agricultural scale.

**Actions:**
- Assign dedicated regional sales teams to Telangana and MP districts
- Partner with local Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) for farmer outreach
- Customise service mix — MP farmers show preference for Net House and Consultancy

---

## (d) Improve Operational Planning for Peak Seasons

Polyhouse and Fan & Pad installations create the highest workload pressure — 28–35 days per project — and tend to cluster in specific seasons.

**Actions:**
- Pre-plan material procurement for Rabi season (Fan & Pad demand) 60 days in advance
- Build a project pipeline dashboard to visualise installation workload by month
- Hire seasonal labour contractors for peak installation periods (June–September)

---

## (e) Leverage Subsidy Timelines Strategically

Government subsidy cycles drive demand spikes. The project growth chart shows peaks corresponding to subsidy scheme launches.

**Actions:**
- Monitor state government subsidy announcements and align sales campaigns to coincide
- Educate farmers on subsidy application processes to reduce friction and increase project closure rates
- Track subsidy utilisation rates by district to identify where farmer awareness is low

---

## (f) Build Recurring Revenue from Maintenance Contracts

Maintenance services contribute ~25–30% of revenue at low operational cost and high frequency.

**Actions:**
- Automatically offer Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMC) at the time of infrastructure installation
- Create a maintenance renewal reminder system 30 days before AMC expiry
- Analyse maintenance non-renewal rates to identify at-risk customer relationships

---

## Summary

| Priority | Action | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Target medium farmers with bundled packages | Revenue uplift ~15–20% |
| 2 | Consultancy-to-infrastructure conversion programme | New pipeline generation |
| 3 | Expand Telangana and MP market presence | New geography revenue |
| 4 | Seasonal operational planning | Reduce project delays |
| 5 | Subsidy-aligned marketing calendar | Demand timing advantage |
| 6 | Maintenance AMC automation | Recurring revenue stability |
