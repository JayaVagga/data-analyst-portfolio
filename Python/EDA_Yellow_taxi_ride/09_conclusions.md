## Key Findings Summary

| Dimension | Key Finding | Business Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Temporal | Peak: 3–7 PM daily; Friday weekly; May/Oct monthly | Time-index fleet deployment and surge pricing |
| Financial | Distance strongest fare predictor (r=0.87); tip correlates with distance (r=0.61) | Incentivise long-distance trips; service quality training |
| Geographical | Midtown Manhattan dominates pickups; airports are high-value zones | Zone-specific deployment schedules |
| Operational | Night (11PM–5AM) = 17% revenue but higher per-trip value | Maintain night fleet near entertainment zones and airports |
| Pricing | Short trips most expensive per mile; both vendors follow same tier pattern | Short-trip flat rates and long-distance incentive packages |
| Customer | Group rides tip more; late-night passengers more generous | Service quality focus on 2–4 passenger and late-night trips |
| Surcharges | Near-universal surcharge application across all hours | Transparent pricing communication to reduce disputes |

---

## (a) Routing & Dispatching Optimisation

- **Peak-hour concentration:** Deploy maximum fleet 6–9 AM and 3–7 PM on weekdays. Adjust weekend schedules for later-starting, broader demand
- **Zone-based routing:** Pre-position cabs near Penn Station and Midtown at 8 AM. Rotate to airport corridors at 4 PM
- **Night operations:** Maintain reduced fleet near entertainment districts and JFK/LGA from 10 PM–3 AM
- **Slow route avoidance:** Use speed-per-route analysis to reroute drivers away from consistently congested zone pairs during peak hours
- **Demand forecasting:** Historical hourly and monthly patterns enable driver shift pre-scheduling 2–4 weeks ahead

---

## (b) Strategic Cab Positioning

- **Midtown Manhattan priority:** Top 10 pickup zones are all in central Manhattan — full fleet presence essential during business hours
- **Airport zones:** JFK, LGA, EWR show high average trip values. Dedicated airport queuing with driver incentives recommended
- **Entertainment zones:** Position 20–30% of night fleet near nightlife clusters (lower Manhattan, Hell's Kitchen) from 9 PM
- **Outer borough growth:** Low pickup/dropoff ratios signal underserved demand. Test targeted promotions in Brooklyn and Queens
- **Residential dropoffs:** Ensure cabs returning from night residential dropoffs can efficiently re-enter active pickup zones

---

## (c) Data-Driven Pricing Adjustments

- **Dynamic surge pricing:** Implement 1.2–1.5x multipliers during peak hours (3–7 PM weekdays, weekend nights)
- **Short-trip flat rates:** Fixed-price options for high-frequency short routes (Penn Station → Midtown hotel corridor)
- **Group ride discount:** 3–5 passenger group discounts of 10–15% per person to compete with ride-hailing apps
- **Long-distance incentive:** Airport and >10 mile trips at fixed competitive rates to attract high-value journeys
- **Vendor price alignment:** Harmonise fare structures across vendors for identical routes and times

---

## Final Statement

The analysis of 2023 NYC Yellow Taxi data identifies **temporal concentration, geographic clustering, and pricing transparency** as the three most actionable levers for operational improvement.

The single highest-impact intervention is **time-indexed fleet deployment** — matching driver supply to the demand patterns identified across hourly, daily, and monthly dimensions. Combined with zone-specific positioning and dynamic pricing, these strategies improve both revenue per trip and driver utilisation without requiring significant capital investment.
